The Biggest Mistakes in Backtesting Strategies

May 10, 2026

Mistaking history for proof

The biggest mistake in backtesting is treating a good historical result as proof. A backtest is evidence, but it is weak evidence if the strategy was designed by repeatedly searching for the best-looking past pattern.

The more choices you test, the easier it becomes to find something that worked by accident.

Data leakage

Data leakage happens when the strategy uses information that would not have been available at the time. It can be subtle: revised data, delayed market resolution, future rankings, or signals calculated with knowledge from the full dataset.

Leakage makes a strategy look smarter than it really is. In live trading, the hidden future disappears.

Ignoring transaction costs

Many strategies look profitable before fees, spread, slippage, and market impact. The more frequently a strategy trades, the more costs matter. A small edge can vanish once execution becomes realistic.

Unrealistic fills

A backtest may assume trades happen at clean prices. Real orders wait, slip, partially fill, or move the market. In prediction markets or small markets, liquidity can be the entire problem.

The goal is not to make the backtest beautiful. The goal is to make it honest enough to be useful.
